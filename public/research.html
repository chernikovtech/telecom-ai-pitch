Strategy consulting & research firms
McKinsey & Company
February 2026
Telcos' AI inflection point: What leaders do to capture value
Based on a December 2025 survey of 49 telecom CxOs globally. Key finding: most telcos have failed to generate real value from fragmented AI pilots, but forward-thinking operators are showing how agentic AI can be a paradigm shift. McKinsey notes that telecom revenue grew just 1% from 2010–2023 despite 60% annual growth in mobile data traffic.
Read on McKinsey.com
McKinsey & Company
November 2025
Building data centers for Africa's unique market dynamics
Projects that Africa's top 5 data center markets will grow from approximately 400 MW today to 1.5–2.2 GW by 2030. Identifies major bottlenecks in power, fiber, and financing. Notes that McKinsey's model anticipates AI as a significant demand accelerator for African DC capacity.
Read on McKinsey.com
BCG (Boston Consulting Group)
February 2026
The AI-First Telecommunications Strategy
BCG argues that telco CEOs must move beyond cost-cutting AI pilots to an enterprise-wide strategy focused on value creation. BCG's 10-20-70 framework emphasizes that algorithms account for only 10% of AI success — the rest is data (20%) and organizational change (70%).
Read on BCG.com
Deloitte
June 2025
Agentic AI Blueprint for Telcos — $150B value unlock
Deloitte's blueprint helps telecom organizations scale and accelerate AI transformation. Estimates $150 billion in value can be unlocked through agentic AI across network management, service delivery, and customer care. Advocates "split inference" for privacy-first on-device processing.
Read press release
Stelia / Big Five Consultancy Analysis
April 2025
Big Five Consultancies Shape the Future of Distributed AI Inference
Analysis of how McKinsey, PwC, BCG, Deloitte, and KPMG are approaching distributed AI inference. McKinsey projects 60–70% of all AI workloads will be real-time inference by 2030. PwC advocates a three-tiered portfolio approach. Deloitte forecasts 30% of smartphones and 50% of PCs will have local AI processing by end of 2025.
Read on Stelia.ai
NVIDIA & industry leadership
NVIDIA — GTC 2025 Keynote
March 2025
Jensen Huang: $100B annual radio network capex will be "accelerated computing infused with AI"
Jensen Huang announced NVIDIA's partnership with Cisco, T-Mobile, and Cerberus ODC to build AI-native radio networks. He stated that annual global capex in radio networks exceeds $100 billion, and this will inevitably shift to accelerated computing with embedded AI. NVIDIA also introduced the Aerial RAN computer for AI-native 6G networks.

NVIDIA — GTC 2026
March 2026
$1 trillion in NVIDIA revenue from 2025–2027; computing demand up 1 million times
At GTC 2026, Huang surveyed the accelerated computing ecosystem across automotive, financial services, healthcare, industrial, media, quantum, retail, robotics, and telecom. He cited $150 billion in AI venture investment and noted computing demand has increased approximately 1 million times over recent years.

NVIDIA — Computex 2025 Keynote
May 2025
"AI is now infrastructure... these AI data centers are AI factories that produce tokens"
Jensen Huang at Computex 2025 declared AI infrastructure will be worth trillions of dollars. He described AI data centers as "factories" that convert energy into tokens — the building blocks of AI value. This reframes the telecom opportunity: telecoms can own these token factories.

Reports that hyperscalers are budgeting nearly $700 billion in capex for 2026. Meta plans up to $135 billion, Google up to $185 billion. NVIDIA's revenue increased 73% to $68.1 billion in Q4 2025. Telecom & market intelligence
GSMA Intelligence
August 2025
Telcos drive AI deployments for cost savings, gear up for revenue growth
Analysis of 250 operators globally. 75–80% of AI deployments target cost cuts; only 10–20% focus on revenue generation. Early revenue-driving use cases like GPU-as-a-Service and agentic AI are expected to grow in 2025–2026. About 60% of deployments are live. Asia Pacific leads with ~4 projects per operator.
Read summary
Global Data Center Hub
January 2026
Middle East & Africa: $100B+ AI and data center buildout
Documents $100B+ in AI and data center commitments across the Gulf, North Africa, and select Sub-Saharan markets. Telecom-led platforms are gaining relevance relative to pure-play developers — control over fiber, landing stations, and regulated infrastructure gives telcos structural advantages.
Read on Global Data Center Hub
Datacenters.com
2025
Emerging data center markets: Southeast Asia and Africa
Analysts forecast over $100 billion in emerging market data center investment by 2030. Identifies Jakarta, Nairobi, Accra, and Lagos as key growth cities. Notes that sovereign funds are co-investing in sovereign cloud infrastructure as part of national AI strategies. Over 270 million people in Indonesia alone creating urgent demand.

U.S. International Trade Administration
2025
Indonesia ICT market: $13.4B telecom, $3.79B data center by 2030
Indonesia's telecom market valued at approximately $13.4 billion in 2025. Data center market projected to reach $3.79 billion by 2030 at 7.99% CAGR. Three major operators — Telkomsel, Indosat, and XLSmart — dominate. Jakarta is the primary DC hub with Batam emerging as a strategic alternative.
