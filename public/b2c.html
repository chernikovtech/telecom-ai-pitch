B2C Platform Strategy

Be the AI app store.
Not the app builder.

Provide the rails — billing, distribution, inference — and let thousands of developers build the AI services your consumers will use. You take 20–30% of every transaction. This is the M-Pesa playbook, applied to AI.

Build your AI platform See the model

The Lesson

Telecoms are not product companies. Stop building apps.

Every time a telco tried to build consumer products in-house, it lost to third parties who moved faster, iterated more, and understood users better. The track record speaks for itself.

01
Carrier app stores
Lost to Google Play & Apple
Vodafone, Orange, and others launched carrier app stores. All dead. Third-party developers chose the platform with the largest reach.
02
Carrier music / video
Lost to Spotify & YouTube
Telecom-built media services could not match the content investment or UX iteration speed of pure-play platforms.
03
Carrier messaging
Lost to WhatsApp
RCS arrived 10 years too late. WhatsApp won because it shipped fast, iterated daily, and did not need committee approval.

The Exception

But M-Pesa won. Why?

Because Safaricom did not try to build every financial product. It built the platform — the rails, the agent network, the billing layer — and let 100,000+ developers and merchants build on top via Daraja API. The result: 60,000+ integrations, $500B+ in annual transaction volume, and 44% of service revenue from VAS.

The playbook is clear: build the infrastructure, provide the distribution, take the platform fee. Now apply it to AI.

The Choice

Build apps or build the platform?

  • Must understand every vertical (education, health, finance...)
  • Linear revenue — scales with headcount
  • Competes with thousands of nimble startups

Winning strategy

  • 20-30% take rate on every transaction
  • History: M-Pesa, App Store, Google Play, Shopify

Network Effects

The platform flywheel

Building your own apps gives you linear growth. A platform gives you compounding network effects — the same loop that made M-Pesa, the App Store, and Shopify dominant.

1
Telecom launches AI developer platform with local inference and carrier billing API
You invest once in the infrastructure and the developer portal. This is the seed.
2
Developers build AI mini-apps for local markets (tutors, health, agri, finance)
They come because you offer what no hyperscaler can: carrier billing, USSD access, mobile money settlement, and 100M+ users.
3
Consumers discover and pay for AI services — via airtime, mobile money, or bundled tokens
No credit card, no app download. Frictionless. 800M+ unbanked users can participate.
4
More users attract more developers. More developers attract more users.
This is the compounding loop. Every new developer makes the platform more valuable for consumers, and vice versa.
5
All inference flows through your GPUs. All billing flows through your ledger.
You take 20-30% of every AI transaction in your market. And you control the infrastructure.
